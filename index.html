Mission

We are dedicated to preserving the rich culinary traditions of Ecuador, making it simple for you to recreate the comforting, vibrant dishes that bring families and communities together. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner, our goal is to inspire you to experience the joy of preparing and sharing these time-honored meals, connecting you to the warmth and culture of Ecuador, one dish at a time. Ecuador

Ecuador is a country with diverse landscapes and climates, which is reflected in its rich and varied culinary traditions. The country is divided into four main geographical regions: the Coast, The Highlands, the Amazon, and the Galapagos Islands. Each region has its own unique ingredients, techniques, and food culture based on its geography, climate, and indigenous traditions

The Coast

The coastal region of Ecuador is known for its tropical climate and fertile soil, making it ideal for growing a variety of fruits, vegetables, and seafood. Being close to the Pacific Ocean, seafood plays a prominent role in coastal cuisine. Their key ingredients are:Fish (like tilapia, sea bass, and shrimp), plantains, corn, coconut, peanuts, bananas, and tropical fruits like mango, guava, and pineapple.

The Highlands

The Andean region, known as the Sierra, is the mountainous heart of Ecuador. The cooler climate here has shaped a cuisine that relies more heavily on root vegetables, grains, and meats. Livestock farming, particularly of cows, pigs, and llamas, is common in this area.Their key ingredients are: Potatoes, corn, quinoa, beans, cheese, pork, beef, and native grains like barley. The region also has a variety of tubers, including mashua and oca.

The Amazon

Ecuador’s Amazon region is a vast area of tropical rainforest, home to many indigenous communities with distinct food traditions. The cuisine here is influenced by the natural abundance of the forest and its biodiversity.Their key ingredients are:Fish (especially river fish), yuca, plantains, jungle fruits, wild meats (such as venison), and native herbs.

The Galapagos Islands

The Galápagos Islands are located in the Pacific Ocean and are known for their unique biodiversity, including marine life. The cuisine here is influenced by the islands' isolation and reliance on sustainable, locally sourced ingredients. Many dishes focus on fresh fish, seafood, and island-grown produce.Their key ingredients are: Fresh seafood (particularly lobster and fish), corn, plantains, and exotic fruits such as passion fruit and bananas.
